What was your previous knowledge of HTML/CSS/Javascript?
Before taking PROG 109, I had no experience or any knowledge of HTML/CSS/Javascript.

Did you use a template or did you build your portfolio from scratch?
I built my portfolio from scratch.

What was your most challenging part of your portfolio (ie, the template, artifacts, CSS, etc.)
Definitely CSS. For each page, I had to find the right size and alignment to make my page look neat. I also had to find colors that would match well with the page. Thus, designing my webpage was very challenging.

How taking this class changed your view on Web Development?
I learned that creating web page isn't easy as I thought. It requires lot of knowledge both in coding and designing. I find Web Development as somewhat similar to fashion designing. You need to have good view of colors, alignments, and details. Furthermore, you need to know coding, which makes Web Development a little more challenging than just programming class.

What would you recommend to future students taking this class?
I would highly recommend future students to get an idea of what Web Development is before taking this class. If I had known about how Web Development works, I wouldn't have struggled too much. Furthermore, I would tell them to study examples of simple web pages they find on internet, such as people's blog or personal portfolios.
